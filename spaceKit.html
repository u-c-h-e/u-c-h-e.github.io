Rejoice, Disorganized Workers: This Smart Cloud Looks After Your Files For You

Cloud storage service, spaceKit, offers baked-in organizing support

Our team of two included a tech lead and myself with three executives from Macys.com for this conceptual project. I facilitated ideation, customer development and led interaction design.

Activities:

Facilitating workshops

Experiment planning

Customer Development

Messaging (copywriting)

Pattern creation

Visual Design

HOUSTON, WE HAVE A PROBLEM

Cloud storage services only offer users a limited amount of features to help with file organization

Folders are helpful, but I asked the question how could we improve cloud storage cloud storage organization for users that could use a bit of help managing their files?

SOLUTION

Better control wrangling various content

After doing that,better vision of the type of user I wanted to “solve” for, I begun brainstorming the solutions a new cloud storage app should address:

  1. Ensuring that dashboards remain clutter-free;
  2. Encouraging users to organize their content in a helpful and unobtrusive manner;
  3. Establishing an easy-to-understand hierarchy for saving content

ASSUMPTIONS

Setting eyes on the target

When it comes to files storage, most people fit into one of these groups:

  1. The Minimalist - uses Cloud storage to back-up essential files only.
  2. The Collector - uses Cloud storage for everything.
  3. The InBetweener - uses Cloud storage for a moderate amount of files.

My focus (and empathy) was with the In-Betweeners, as they generally have a wealth of content and sometimes find it difficult to keep track of everything. Knowing this, I wanted to create a versatile app that would help them organize their content in a streamlined and intuitive way.
