ASSUMPTIONS

Setting eyes on the target

When it comes to files storage, most people fit into one of these groups:

  1. The Minimalist - uses Cloud storage to back-up essential files only.
  2. The Collector - uses Cloud storage for everything.
  3. The InBetweener - uses Cloud storage for a moderate amount of files.

My focus (and empathy) was with the In-Betweeners, as they generally have a wealth of content and sometimes find it difficult to keep track of everything. Knowing this, I wanted to create a versatile app that would help them organize their content in a streamlined and intuitive way.

PROCESS

Adding some meat to the skeletons

My user survey participants may have provided the foundation—or better, skeletons—for my personas. But the two 30-minute remote interviews with survey participants provided the meat and skin I used to real bring those personas to life.

Meet Quandra, the Constant Collaborator

Goals

I want to be able track document changes and see live edits with a group of people. I’d like to also save online article links somewhere I can quickly access to read later.


Frustrations

I feel that there are too organizational features left up to the users to decide. It would be helpful to have a way to better organize files and whatnot.


Motivation

I use cloud storage to make sure sharing documents is seamless and painless, and to keep track the various projects I'm a part of.
