Connecting Sex Trafficking Survivors to Resources

How might we empower human trafficking survivors to rebuild their lives?

As co-designer, I led the brainstorming, created wireframes, and collaboratively contributed to the visual design for this hackathon project.

My roles:

Design Sprint Facilitation

Competitive Analysis

Accessibility Advocacy

Wireframing & User Flows

Visual Design

Problem

Survivors of human trafficking need a central platform to connect to local resources and build community.

While resources exist to support them, those resources can be dispersed and require users looking for support having to visit several online resources to access all the information and help they need.

Solution

A Supportive Platform for Human Traffic Survivors

Rise offers survivors a judgement-free, empathic support system and community. It empowers members to rebuild their lives through Rise's network and connects them to local resources to support their goals.

Process

Clarifying Product Requirements

This app was the idea of the software developer, Ayesha, so the rest of the team depended on her existing research to help shape the product requirements. During the brainstorming phase, I decided to step up as facilitator to avoid us from jumping directly into designing a solution without having a clear vision of who the product was meant for and the expected outcomes.

Reflection

Next Steps

Within the timeline of this project, our team had to rely on secondary research. Given more time on the project, I would like to dive deeper into research and conduct user interviews and surveys to validate or invalidate initial assumptions. I'd also recommend the team further explore the idea of creating a chatbot to decrease the barrier to entry for a new member accessing Rise for the first time.
