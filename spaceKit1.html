Rejoice, Disorganized Workers: This Cloud Service Helps Keep Your Dashboard Clutter-Free

How might we update the cloud storage experience to encourage better content organization?

As design lead, I led the ideation, user research, and UX design for this conceptual, solo project.

HOUSTON, WE HAVE A PROBLEM

Cloud storage services only offer users a limited amount of features to help with file organization

While cloud storage is expected to continue growing, providers have not yet focused on helping users who struggle with keeping the increasing number of files they store on the cloud organized.

SOLUTION

Better control wrangling various content

I started off with a few assumptions pertaining to the target user. After fleshing out a better vision of the type of user I wanted to “solve” for through research, I begun brainstorming the specific solutions a new cloud storage app should address:

  1. Ensuring that dashboards remain clutter-free;
  2. Encouraging users to organize their content in a helpful and unobtrusive manner;
  3. Establishing an easy-to-understand hierarchy for saving content

ASSUMPTIONS

Setting eyes on the target

When it comes to files storage, most people fit into one of these groups:

  1. The Minimalist - uses Cloud storage to back-up essential files only.
  2. The Collector - uses Cloud storage for everything.
  3. The InBetweener - uses Cloud storage for a moderate amount of files.

My focus (and empathy) was with the In-Betweeners, as they generally have a wealth of content and sometimes find it difficult to keep track of everything. Knowing this, I wanted to create a versatile app that would help them organize their content in a streamlined and intuitive way.

USER TESTING

Folder images customization

Additional options for folder covers: I also received feedback that it would be helpful to have additional options to customize their folder covers. Originally, option A was to drag and drop an image file to the front of the folder they wanted to update. The alternative Option B was to right-click the bottom of the folder card and select ‘Edit folder image’ from the dropdown menu.

Before: Right-clicking to edit folder image

Auto-populated banner images & stock: For users without a particular image in mind to use for a folder cover, there needed to be a way to help them select a cover without it feeling like a pesky additional step. The solution was to have a modal appear after users create a folder where they could also chose:

  • an image from the folder they uploaded, or

  • a photo from one of spaceKit’s free stock, or

  • a color for their folder.


Modal providing users with options for editing folder covers

LESSON #1

Poor direction is the biggest mistake you could make

Okay, so that probably

got your attention.

The importance of having a clear idea and vision from the beginning of a large project cannot be overstated. When I started this project, I was biased in thinking that there wasn't much that could be improved in Cloud storage.



Later in the process, I realized I was wrong. I eventually found the right direction, but lost valuable time in the process. I learned to focus more time on the research stage, in formulating and asking the right questions to ensure I was heading in the right direction.

LESSON #2

Surveys are helpful, but only when you have (proper) direction

Tied to lesson one, a symptom of having poor direction is not knowing the right questions to ask . If I could do this project over, I would have created a secondary survey to ask respondents about their specific behaviors and preferences when it comes to organizing content. Now I spend more time trying to understand problems users face before I begin working out solutions.

×
LESSON #3

Have a key differentiator

My differentiator came to me at a later stage, after interacting with usability testers and having one-on-one conversations with them. I learned that pretty design is not enough to engage users and create value for them. If there isn't something unique about your product that none of your competitors already have, it is necessary to go back to square one.
